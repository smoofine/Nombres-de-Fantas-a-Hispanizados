# D&D 5e Expanded Multiverse: Cultural Lore, Naming Structures, and Inspirations

This reference catalog compiles the cultural background, naming conventions, and real-world inspirations for the comprehensive list of official, setting-specific, and major third-party expansion races. It isolates linguistic and cultural identity to assist LLMs in adapting and localizing names into a natural Spanish phonetic framework.

---

## 1. Aarakocra
*   **Naming Conventions:** Names are brief, sharp, and highly insectoid or avian, consisting largely of clicks, chirps, and whistling consonants ($B, C, K, Q$). They can be difficult for other species to pronounce without a beak.
    *   *Examples:* Aera, Ikki, Kleeck, Oorr, Quierk.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Native American avian mythology (the Thunderbird) blended with the physical phonetics of birds of prey.
*   **Culture Summary:** Avian humanoids native to the Elemental Plane of Air. They are nomadic sky-dwellers who value absolute freedom, change, and open spaces. They have little concept of borders or material property, focusing their lives on hunting and guarding the skies against earthbound threats.

## 2. Aasimar (Court, Fallen, Protector, Scourge, Seeker)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Usually given standard human names matching their local culture at birth. As they mature and connect with their Celestial Guide, many adopt majestic, vowel-heavy **Celestial names** featuring resonant, angelic suffixes like *-el* or *-on*.
    *   *Examples:* Azael, Imshael, Malandros, Sariel, Valerius.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Biblical angelology, Christian mystics, and Abrahamic mythology.
*   **Culture Summary:** Mortals touched by the cosmic light of the Upper Planes. Their culture varies by subrace: Protectors pursue peace and defense; Scourges burn with a radiant, aggressive desire to purge evil; Seekers study divine mysteries; and Fallen Aasimar have internal shadows that twist their light into necrotic energy. They are often solitary figures driven by prophetic dreams.

## 3. Astral Elf
*   **Naming Conventions:** Melodic and soft like traditional elven names, but layered with archaic, starlit, or cold planar phonetics. They often favor long, open trailing vowels that suggest timelessness.
    *   *Examples:* Althaea, Myseril, Naivara, Xael.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** High-fantasy space opera archetypes combined with ancient cosmic deities from Greco-Roman myth.
*   **Culture Summary:** Elves who left the material world millennia ago to dwell in the Astral Plane. Because the Astral Plane is timeless, their culture is frozen, deeply philosophical, and melancholic. They spend centuries mastering single crafts or artistic disciplines, viewing mortal timelines with detached curiosity.

## 4. Autognome
*   **Naming Conventions:** Names are typically highly practical factory designations, serial codes, or whimsical names assigned to them by their rock gnome creators. Many adopt short, functional nouns when traveling with mortals.
    *   *Examples:* Click, Gearbox, Rusty, Sprocket, Unit 4-B.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Clockwork automatons from European fairy tales (e.g., Pinocchio, Tin Woodman) and industrial-era mechanical toys.
*   **Culture Summary:** Sentient clockwork beings built by eccentric gnomes. Their culture is centered around their programming and their creators' legacy. They possess a deep joy for routine maintenance, clockwork engineering, and collecting small, shiny mechanical parts, treating friendship as a permanent, logical directive.

## 5. Barding, Men of Bree, and Rangers of the North
*   **Naming Conventions:** 
    *   *Bardings:* Favor harsh, Old Norse and traditional Germanic linguistic structures.
    *   *Men of Bree:* Use simple, rustic Anglo-Saxon given names paired with descriptive, earthy surnames based on wild flora or local geography.
    *   *Rangers:* Use archaic, dignified Westron or Sindarin-influenced elven names.
    *   *Examples:* Brand, Bain (Barding); Barnaby Apples, Tom Heathertoes (Bree); Aragorn, Halbarad (Ranger).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** The early medieval European and pastoral English settings of J.R.R. Tolkien’s Middle-earth.
*   **Culture Summary:** Distinct human societies. Bardings are proud, dragon-slaying northmen focused on rebuilding grand trading kingdoms. Men of Bree are quiet, insular, comfort-loving townspeople integrated closely with halflings. Rangers are a secretive, stoic, nomadic diaspora dedicated to silently protecting the wilderness from ancient evils.

## 6. Bearfolk (incl. Shadowborn)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Names are heavy, booming, and filled with broad vowels ($A, O, U$) and thick consonant clusters ($Br, Gr, Tr$) to evoke the raw strength of a bear's roar.
    *   *Examples:* Bjorn, Gurn, Thorgar, Vorn.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Slavic, Scandinavian, and Siberian shape-shifter folklore (such as the Beornings of epic fantasy).
*   **Culture Summary:** Massive ursine humanoids who value physical strength, honor, and raw survival. Shadowborn variants have been altered by the ecology of planar shadows, giving them a more reclusive, fatalistic outlook. Their society is tribal and deeply tied to the seasons, alternating between active hunting cycles and quiet communal periods.

## 7. Beastfolk
*   **Naming Conventions:** Rely on short, onomatopoeic names or literal, animalistic descriptions translated into simple common words.
    *   *Examples:* Claw, Fang, Swift, Tracker.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Universal therianthropic myths and tribal animism.
*   **Culture Summary:** A diverse, highly adaptive umbrella race of animal-hybrid humanoids. They reject complex city infrastructures, choosing instead to live in migratory packs or druidic circles that balance hunting instincts with ancestral spirit worship.

## 8. Bree-Hobbit / Hobbit (Harfoot, Fallohide, Stoor)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Simple, cheerful given names with soft consonant sounds, combined with compound, descriptive English country surnames that highlight comfort, agriculture, or physical traits.
    *   *Examples:* Peregrin, Bilbo (Given); Proudfoot, Greenbottle, Underhill (Surnames).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** The quintessential nineteenth-century rural English countryside lifestyle.
*   **Culture Summary:** Small, agrarian humanoids who value peace, culinary excellence, smoking pipes, and community harmony above all else. Harfoots are pastoral and subterranean; Fallohides are taller, more adventurous, and fond of languages; Stoors prefer waterways, fishing, and muddy terrains.

## 9. Bugbear (incl. Dhakaani Guul'dar)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Standard bugbears use blunt, guttural, single-syllable names ($Grak, Klarg$). However, the ancient **Dhakaani bugbears (Guul'dar)** use highly formal, sharp military titles and structured clan names emphasizing personal duty.
    *   *Examples:* Thruun, General Morak, Jin'grak.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Traditional European Bogeyman folklore mixed with Imperial military clan societies.
*   **Culture Summary:** Ambush predators. Standard bugbears live on stealth and intimidation. In contrast, the Dhakaani subrace adheres to an intense, ancient honor system (*muut* and *chib*), serving as disciplined vanguard shock troops within subterranean military legions.

## 10. Centaur
*   **Naming Conventions:** Melodic, sweeping names that combine open vowels with long, rolling consonants ($L, R, N, V$) to evoke the sound of open plains and running hooves.
    *   *Examples:* Chon, Dunva, Hadros, Niles.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Greco-Roman classical mythology and nomadic Eurasian steppe horse cultures.
*   **Culture Summary:** Proud, migratory herd-dwellers closely linked to the rhythms of nature. They prioritize the freedom of the sprint and view civilization's stone walls with deep pity and suspicion.

## 11. Cervan (Grove, Pronghorn)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Dignified, earthbound names that use soft sibilants and natural pauses, creating a rhythm that sounds like rustling leaves or wind through branches.
    *   *Examples:* Elan, Corven, Mirna, Thawn.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Sylvan wildlife lore and Celtic forest deities (Cernunnos).
*   **Culture Summary:** Deer-like humanoids from woodland canopies. Grove cervans are deeply spiritual, intuitive, and protective of hidden forest secrets. Pronghorn cervans are physically imposing, building stoic, defensive frontier settlements to protect their family lines from external predators.

## 12. Changeling
*   **Naming Conventions:** Their true names are brief, sharp, and monosyllabic, using soft consonants and fluid vowels that are easy to pronounce regardless of the physical form they assume.
    *   *Examples:* Bin, Cas, Dox, Hix, Nyx, Vox.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** European fairy folklore (the fae double) and psychological archetypes of the social mask.
*   **Culture Summary:** Shapechangers who navigate life by altering their physical appearance. Their culture revolves around identity management: "Passers" completely bury their true nature to blend into human society, while "Becomers" treat identity as an evolving art piece, constantly rotating through distinct persona names and lifestyles.

## 13. Cnidaran (Nematocyst, Shimmerskin)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Sibilant, whispering names full of soft $S, Sh,$ and fluid $L$ sounds, mimicking the motion of drifting ocean currents.
    *   *Examples:* Ssilis, Lyssha, Shimmer, Ness.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Marine biology (cnidarians/jellyfish) and deep-sea bioluminescent biolore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Translucent, jellyfish-like humanoids. Nematocysts focus on chemical defense and hunting along coastal reefs, while Shimmerskins use their shifting, bioluminescent hides for high-society court manipulation and mesmerizing diplomatic displays.

## 14. Corvum (Dusk, Kindled)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Sharp, clipped, and severe. They lean heavily on hard clicks, scratchy sounds ($C, K, X$), and dry phonetics that mirror the caw of a crow.
    *   *Examples:* Kael, Corvus, Varek, Xun.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Avian folklore regarding corvids (ravens/crows) as symbols of intellect, magic, and ill omen.
*   **Culture Summary:** Crow-like humanoids who value intellect, legal bureaucracy, and arcane knowledge. Dusk corvums operate out of urban shadows as cutthroat investigators or spies; Kindled corvums are ambitious, charismatic mages and political leaders who use their wit to climb societal hierarchies.

## 15. Cyclopian
*   **Naming Conventions:** Large, archaic, and thunderous names drawing from primitive Latinate or ancient Mediterranean root words.
    *   *Examples:* Balor, Steropes, Brontes, Oculon.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Homeric epic poetry and Bronze Age Greek mythological giants.
*   **Culture Summary:** One-eyed giants who value isolated craftsmanship, architectural engineering, and mountain stonemasonry. Their society is built around deep subterranean forges and geometric architecture.

## 16. Darakhul (incl. Bearfolk/Shadow Goblin Heritages)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Names are either remnants of the mortal name they held before dying, or short, skeletal, biting titles chosen to reflect their undead state.
    *   *Examples:* Ghoulmaster, Bonesplitter, Korvath, Morana.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Dark-fantasy ghoul empires and Gothic undead aristocracy.
*   **Culture Summary:** Sentient, civilized ghouls who maintain an underground empire. Their culture is deeply structured, highly political, and chillingly pragmatic—they view all living humanoids primarily as livestock, but maintain high art, law, and diplomacy within their own dark palaces.

## 17. Dhampir
*   **Naming Conventions:** Retain the naming conventions of their mortal birth culture, but often adopt dark, aristocratic, or archaic aliases to mask their true nature while wandering among normal populations.
    *   *Examples:* Alucard, Carmilla, Valerius, Von.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Eastern European vampire folklore and Gothic horror literature.
*   **Culture Summary:** Humanoids caught between life and undeath, driven by a supernatural hunger (for blood, raw flesh, or psychic energy). They have no true civilization of their own; they live as isolated predators, elite hunters, or cursed wanderers on the fringes of mortal societies.

## 18. Dragonborn (Chromatic, Gem, Metallic, Draconblood, Ravenite)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Names feature harsh, explosive sounds, sharp consonants ($K, R, Th$), and long vowels. Crucially, they place their **Clan Name first** as a matter of supreme social status, followed by their personal name.
    *   *Clan Names:* Clethtinthiallor, Daardendrian, Myastan.
    *   *Personal Names:* Arjhan, Balasar, Kriv, Surina.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Mesoamerican linguistics layered over Feudal Japanese bushido honor structures.
*   **Culture Summary:** Proud reptilian humanoids devoted to clan honor. Draconbloods are wealthy, manipulative aristocrats; Ravenites are fiercely independent, practical warriors; Chromatic, Metallic, and Gem lineages define their philosophical alignments and elemental pride.

## 19. Dwarf (Blue Mountain, Erebor, Iron Hill, Ruinbound, Wandering, Mark of Warding)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Dictated by clan elders and rooted in ancient family legacy. Names use heavy, metallic consonants ($K, G, D, T, R$) and harsh syllables. Surnames are descriptive compound English phrases.
    *   *Given:* Adrik, Baern, Thoradin.
    *   *Surnames:* Battlehammer, Ironfist, Forjafuego (Fireforge).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Norse mythology, Germanic linguistics, and Tolkienesque subterranean industry.
*   **Culture Summary:** Stoic mountain artisans and miners. Blue Mountain and Erebor dwarves focus on grand architectural restoration; Iron Hill variants are heavily militarized; Ruinbound dwarves battle horrific deep-earth anomalies; Wandering dwarves live as displaced nomadic traders.

## 20. Eladrin
*   **Naming Conventions:** Lyrical and standard elven at their core, but their active surnames and titles shift constantly to reflect their seasonal changes and emotional states.
    *   *Examples:* Autumnwhisper, Summerwrath, Winterheart, Springbloom.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** The mutable, dangerous nature of the high fae in Celtic folklore (Tuatha Dé Danann).
*   **Culture Summary:** Elves native to the Feywild whose physical bodies and personalities morph across four distinct seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) depending on their dominant emotional state. Their culture is an unpredictable tapestry of art, passion, and natural magic.

## 21. Elf (Aereni, Drow, High, Lindon, Lunar, Pallid, Sable, Sea, Shadar-kai, Shadow Fey, Wood)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Lyrical, flowing, and melodic, prioritizing soft consonants and sweeping vowels ($L, S, F, M, Y$). Family names are compound poetic translations of natural or astral concepts.
    *   *Adult Names:* Aramil, Enialis (Male); Anastrianna, Sariel (Female).
    *   *Family Names:* Amakiir (Gemflower), Galanodel (Moonwhisper).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Celtic linguistics, Welsh phonetics, and classic Tolkienesque high fantasy.
*   **Culture Summary:** Long-lived, deeply magical humanoids. Aereni worship their undead ancestors; Drow live in a sibilant, matriarchal Underdark; Lindon, Sea, and Wood elves protect isolated aquatic or sylvan realms; Shadar-kai and Shadow Fey serve planar courts of raw shadow and sorrow.

## 22. Enkoh (Hulking, Springtail)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Feature quick, rhythmic syllables with sudden, sharp stops. Hulking variants favor heavier, blockier sounds, while Springtails use bouncy, clipping phonetics.
    *   *Examples:* Kenji, Taro, Koko, Suki.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** East Asian monkey-king folklore (Sun Wukong) and simian troop behavior.
*   **Culture Summary:** Agile, simian humanoids who value freedom, vertical agility, and trickery. Hulking enkoh act as protective brawlers; Springtails are hyperactive, curious urban scouts who treat cities as structural playgrounds.

## 23. Erina (incl. Spiritfarer)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Short, clicking, and unassuming names, often utilizing double consonants and rustic, soft endings that sound like small animals foraging.
    *   *Examples:* Pippin, Tilba, Grik, Mirri.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** European hedge-animal fables and quiet, pastoral folklore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Hedgehog-like humanoids who live in peaceful, burrowed agricultural communities. Spiritfarer variants have a deep spiritual connection to ancestral souls, serving as gentle guides who bridge the gap between living villages and the spirit world.

## 24. Etherean
*   **Naming Conventions:** Ghostly and light, featuring elongated vowels and sweeping sibilants that sound as if they are being whispered across a vast, empty room.
    *   *Examples:* Valas, Ethra, Phantoma, Zephyrus.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Spiritualist séances, phantom lore, and modern concepts of parallel dimensions.
*   **Culture Summary:** Semi-incorporeal humanoids tied to the Ethereal Plane. Their culture is quiet, observational, and highly scholarly; they live as cosmic historians, drifting through physical barriers to record historical moments without directly interfering.

## 25. Fairy
*   **Naming Conventions:** Intensely whimsical, light, and naming individuals directly after natural objects, pleasant sounds, sensory reactions, or bright flora.
    *   *Examples:* Bramble, Dewdrop, Pip, Thistle, Zephyr.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Victorian fairy tale illustrations and Pan-Celtic folklore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Diminutive fey humanoids who embody absolute personal freedom. They reject codified laws, instead operating via chaotic personal contracts, hospitality pacts, and lighthearted illusions inside pristine, untouched forest ecosystems.

## 26. Feathren
*   **Naming Conventions:** High, musical, and sweeping names that rely on long, soaring vowels ($A, E, I$) and soft, breezy consonants.
    *   *Examples:* Coris, Avia, Feather, Aquila.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Classic aviary high fantasy and sky-island myths.
*   **Culture Summary:** Regal, fully feathered humanoids who build mountaintop or sky-island monasteries. They value theological study, wind-current navigation, and maintaining a detached, elevated view of material political conflicts below.

## 27. Firbolg
*   **Naming Conventions:** They **do not use true names** within their own society, viewing them as unnecessary labels. They refer to each other by tribal roles or descriptive outdoor titles. When traveling with mortals, they adopt soft, elven or pastoral names given to them by neighbors.
    *   *Adopted Titles:* Boulderbreaker, Treefriend, Oakheart.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** The mythological Fir Bolg of ancient Irish history mixed with modern ecological guardian themes.
*   **Culture Summary:** Giant, peaceful forest-dwellers. Their culture is strictly communistic and reclusive—they take only what is absolutely necessary from the woods, protect wildlife using powerful illusion magic, and remain deeply hidden from industrial expansion.

## 28. Fuyoren
*   **Naming Conventions:** Fluid, soft, and deeply poetic, utilizing classical Japanese phonetics to construct names based on changing seasons, traditional arts, or natural elements.
    *   *Examples:* Sakura, Haru, Ren, Yuki.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Japanese Kami (nature spirit) lore and traditional Shinto aesthetics.
*   **Culture Summary:** Graceful, plant-hybrid or floral humanoids. They view life as an artistic cycle of blooming and wilting, spending their time tending ancient shrines, composing poetry, and using sylvan magic to protect sacred natural sites.

## 29. Gallus (Bright, Huden)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Warm, cozy, and rhythmic, reminiscent of rustic, early medieval agricultural communities. Surnames are tied to cooking, farming, or textiles.
    *   *Examples:* Caleb, Mae, Timothy (Given); Wheatley, Barnfield (Surnames).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Traditional European rural farming folk and anthropomorphic fowl folklore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Fowl-like humanoids who form the backbone of agricultural societies. Bright gallus are natural community organizers, priests, and scholars; Huden gallus are deeply practical farmers and trackers who possess an intuitive connection to the soil and crop management.

## 30. Geleton
*   **Naming Conventions:** Slurping, wet, or heavy names, often using repetitive, amorphous syllables that sound like shifting fluids or bubbling chemistry.
    *   *Examples:* Blobbo, Gloop, Slime, Viscus.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Classic pulp sci-fi ooze monsters and alchemical folklore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Sentient, gel-based humanoids. They have no structured cities, instead operating as specialized alchemical cleanup crews or fluid subterranean explorers who can slip through the tightest structural cracks.

## 31. Genasi (Air, Earth, Fire, Water)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Take human names from their mortal parentage but heavily modify or replace them with elements, nouns, or verbs that match their elemental nature.
    *   *Air:* Breeze, Gust, Whish, Zephyr.
    *   *Earth:* Flint, Jasper, Obsidian, Onyx.
    *   *Fire:* Ash, Blaze, Ember, Ignis.
    *   *Water:* Cascade, Ripple, Shore, Torrent.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Middle Eastern Djinn/Genie folklore and classical Greco-Roman elemental philosophies.
*   **Culture Summary:** Humanoids born with the raw magic of the Elemental Planes. They lack a unified civilization, instead adapting to match their inner element: Air genasi are flighty and transient; Earth variants are unyielding and stoic; Fire variants are loud, passionate, and explosive; Water variants are fluid, adaptive, and intensely independent.

## 32. Giff
*   **Naming Conventions:** Formal, stiff, and distinctly traditional. They favor stuffy, upper-class Victorian names paired with grand, bombastic military ranks and titles.
    *   *Examples:* Barnaby, Eustace, Percival, Reginald (preceded by titles like Major, Colonel, or Captain).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** British colonial military structures, black-powder musketeer aesthetics, and hippopotamuses.
*   **Culture Summary:** Broad, hippo-headed humanoids obsessed with military hierarchy, black-powder firearms, and heavy artillery. Their culture is strictly mercenary—they view all planetary conflicts through the lens of contract execution and operational military etiquette, considering an unstructured life to be a tragedy.

## 33. Gith (Githyanki, Githzerai)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Sharp, harsh, and thoroughly alien. They use sharp consonants ($Z, K, V, X$) separated by **glottal stops and apostrophes**. They do not use surnames, using military rank or monastic position instead.
    *   *Examples:* K’rak, Menak, Quorik, Vraasin, Zaerith.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Sci-fi conlangs, Spartan military states (Githyanki), and ascetic Zen Buddhist monasteries (Githzerai).
*   **Culture Summary:** A race liberated from Mind Flayer slavery, split into two factions: Githyanki are ruthless astral pirates living under a lich-queen; Githzerai are highly disciplined, meditative ascetics who use psychic focus to build stable keeps out of the raw chaos of Limbo.

## 34. Gnoll
*   **Naming Conventions:** Guttural, low, and aggressive, filled with snapping consonants, rolling r's, and harsh laughing sounds ($Ch, Kh, Rr$).
    *   *Examples:* Garrow, Harrak, Karr, Rorsh.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Hyena pack behaviors and dark-fantasy marauder tropes.
*   **Culture Summary:** Hyena-like humanoids. While standard multiverse gnolls are driven by demonic bloodlust, specific expanded settings (like Eberron's Znir Pact) show a deeply practical, fiercely loyal mercenary culture that broke away from demonic influence to sell their martial tracking skills to the highest bidder.

## 35. Gnome (Forest, Rock, Mark of Scribing, Wyrd)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Gnomes gather names like treasure, using a massive collection throughout their lives consisting of a given name, parents' names, clan names, and multiple playful nicknames. Names are light, rapid, and slightly absurd.
    *   *Given:* Alston, Fonkin, Roondar (Male); Bimpnottin, Breena (Female).
    *   *Nicknames:* Badger, Cloak, Stumbleduck.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Germanic/Alpine folklore (Nisse/Tomte) and Victorian clockwork engineering.
*   **Culture Summary:** Endlessly curious, small humanoids. Forest gnomes converse with woodland animals; Rock gnomes are master tinkers; Scribing gnomes control magical communication guilds; Wyrd gnomes study fate and temporal anomalies.

## 36. Gobboc
*   **Naming Conventions:** Rapid, high-pitched, and slightly wet, utilizing quick double consonants and snapping endings.
    *   *Examples:* Gribbit, Pok, Zib, Nok.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Amphibian folklore and bog-dwelling fairy tale tropes.
*   **Culture Summary:** Small, frog-like swamp humanoids who build elaborate floating villages. Their culture is centered on communal fishing, bog alchemy, and oral storytelling around peat fires.

## 37. Goblin (incl. Dhakaani Golin'dar Artizans/Kesh'dar)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Standard goblins use brief, screeching names ($Breeg, Nox$). However, the ancient **Dhakaani Goblins (Golin'dar)** use highly organized names that denote their clan function—whether as elite artisans or stealthy spies ($Kesh'dar$).
    *   *Examples:* Droa, Geth, Kaza, Vola.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Traditional European goblin folklore reinvented via an organized, subterranean industrial clan system.
*   **Culture Summary:** Standard goblins are scavenging outcasts. Dhakaani Golin'dar goblins, however, are highly respected architectural backbones of an ancient empire, serving as master builders, tinkers, and intelligence operatives.

## 38. Goliath
*   **Naming Conventions:** Every individual holds a strict three-part name: a birth name given by parents (short and simple), an **earned nickname** given by the tribal chief based on a major exploit or flaw, and a long, ancestral clan name.
    *   *Birth:* Gauthak, Kuori, Orilo, Thulai.
    *   *Nickname:* Bearkiller, Eagle-eye, Flintfinder, Lonehunter.
    *   *Clan:* Anakalathai, Elanithino.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Inuit indigenous tribal structures and Scandinavian mountain survival cultures.
*   **Culture Summary:** Massive mountain-dwelling humanoids who live in competitive, merit-based societies. They maintain a strict internal scoreboard of achievements, despising personal laziness and believing that every single individual must pull their own weight to keep the tribe alive.

## 39. Golynn
*   **Naming Conventions:** Earthy, heavy, and structurally compact, utilizing solid, blocky consonant clusters that mimic the sound of digging through stone or the clatter of heavy armor plating.
    *   *Examples:* Rhyo, Boros, Krad, Thrum.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** The anatomy and defensive posture of the pangolin, blended with deep-earth mining and mineralogical themes.
*   **Culture Summary:** Heavy, subterranean humanoids characterized by a distinct animalistic, crouched posture and a curved spine. Their bodies are covered in thick, protective plates displaying intricate scale patterns that resemble crystalline minerals like rhyolite. Their culture is subterranean, focused on deep-earth excavation, territorial defense, and mineral engineering.

## 40. Grimalkin
*   **Naming Conventions:** Soft, playful, and sibilant, featuring purring or scratching sounds ($Pr, Sh, Cl$) that can change on a whim to suit their mood.
    *   *Examples:* Purr, Scratch, Whisker, Claws.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Feline folklore, Cheshire cat tropes, and trickster fairy tales.
*   **Culture Summary:** Small, cat-like humanoids who live as natural illusionists and nomadic tricksters. They treat life as an open-ended game, avoiding permanent responsibilities and traveling between urban markets to trade secrets, gossip, and luxury goods.

## 41. Hadozee
*   **Naming Conventions:** Nautically flavored, short, and dynamic. They favor practical names that can be yelled across the windy deck of a ship. Surnames are often earned maritime titles.
    *   *Examples:* Gar, Koko, Shipley, Tar, Tucker.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Arboreal primates (gliding squirrels/monkeys) combined with Age of Sail deckhand culture.
*   **Culture Summary:** Simian humanoids with wing-like membranes that allow them to glide. They are natural mariners and spelljammer deckhands who love high-altitude climbing, open oceans, and astronomical navigation, maintaining a fiercely loyal, crew-first family structure.

## 42. Half-Dryad
*   **Naming Conventions:** Combine melodic elven or human given names with permanent sibilant titles derived from the specific sacred tree species of their dryad parent.
    *   *Examples:* Elidon Oak, Sylva Willow, Rowan Bark.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Classical Greek hamadryad myths and Romantic-era nature poetry.
*   **Culture Summary:** Humanoids caught between mortality and sylvan timelessness. They serve as natural ambassadors between growing timber cities and ancient, angry druidic circles, protecting forest borders with subtle plant magic.

## 43. Half-Dwarf
*   **Naming Conventions:** Juxtapose heavy, industrial dwarven clan names with fluid human given names, reflecting their mixed heritage.
    *   *Examples:* John Battlehammer, Mary Ironfist.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Urban melting-pot dynamics and classic fantasy frontier integration.
*   **Culture Summary:** Born of human and dwarven parents, they often act as master industrial architects and merchants in frontier trade cities, blending human ambition and rapid innovation with dwarven structural longevity.

## 44. Half-Elf (Base, Aquatic, Drow, Mark of Detection/Storm)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Caught completely between two worlds; those raised by humans are given lyrical Elven names to emphasize their exotic heritage, while those raised by Elves are given human names.
    *   *Examples:* Laucian (in human towns), David (in elven spires).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Diaspora communities and the psychology of navigating mixed cultural identities.
*   **Culture Summary:** Highly diplomatic humanoids who turn their lack of a permanent homeland into an analytical advantage, serving as master navigators, corporate dragonmarked leaders (Detection/Storm), and universal ambassadors across the multiverse.

## 45. Half-Orc (incl. Mark of Finding)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Rely on short, aggressive Orcish names dominated by guttural consonants ($G, K, R$). Surnames are rare, replaced by earned battlefield descriptors or tracking epithets.
    *   *Examples:* Dench, Henk, Krusk, Tracker (Given); Skullcrusher (Epithet).

*   **Culture Summary:** Draconic/aquatic humanoids. Nishikin variants are brightly colored community leaders and artists; Ryujin variants are proud, martial guardians who protect deep underwater palaces and tidal shrines.

## 97. Tengu (Hanataka, Karasu)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Sharp, rapid, and arrogant, utilizing traditional Japanese yokai naming systems linked to mountain storms, wind, or swordplay.
    *   *Examples:* Tengu, Storm, Wind, Blade.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Japanese Tengu folklore (crow and long-nosed mountain spirits).
*   **Culture Summary:** Avian mountain-dwellers. Karasu are black-feathered crow scouts and spies; Hanataka are long-nosed, fiercely arrogant martial masters who live as hermit sword instructors on isolated mountain peaks.

## 98. The Disembodied
*   **Naming Conventions:** Use the elegant, high-aristocratic names of their lost mortal kingdom, but spoken with a ghostly, hollow echo to reflect their semi-incorporeal physical forms.
    *   *Examples:* Lord Malakor, Lady Seraphina, Charles the Lost.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Phantom royalty, ghost towns, and planar displacement accidents.
*   **Culture Summary:** Survivors of a magical disaster that ripped their city between dimensions. They are semi-incorporeal phantoms who struggle to physically interact with the material world, living inside shifting planar ruins as melancholic historians.

## 99. Thri-kreen
*   **Naming Conventions:** Composed entirely of clicks, buzzes, and snapping mandibles ($X, Tz, Kk$), lacking any soft human vowels. They cannot be easily spoken by species without an insectoid exoskeleton.
    *   *Examples:* T'chazz, Kkrak, Xxik, Tzt.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Desert praying mantis behaviors and insectoid science-fiction conlangs.
*   **Culture Summary:** Six-limbed insectoid desert nomads. They never sleep, possess a telepathic pack bond, and live in a state of constant hunting efficiency, viewing all of existence through the lens of ecological balance and predator-prey dynamics.

## 100. Tieflings (Bloodlines: Asmodeus, Baalzebul, Dispater, Fierna, Glasya, Levistus, Mammon, Mephistopheles, Zariel)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Rely on three main paths: ancestral **Infernal names** full of sharp, diagnostic sibilants ($Akmenos, Mordai$); local human names; or **Virtue names** chosen by the individual to represent a philosophical concept they wish to conquer or embody.
    *   *Virtue Names:* Creed (Credo), Hope (Esperanza), Torment (Tormento), Art (Arte).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Abrahamic demonology and Puritan/Quaker abstract noun names.
*   **Culture Summary:** Humanoids bearing fiendish bloodlines. They face severe societal prejudice, leading them to become fiercely self-reliant criminals, dark mages, or determined heroes. Their fiendish subrace determines their unique magical heritage (e.g., Mammon tieflings favor wealth magic; Zariel variants manifest martial fire).

## 101. Tortle
*   **Naming Conventions:** Brief, gender-neutral, and soft, utilizing rounded vowels and rolling consonants ($L, R, T$) that rarely exceed two syllables. They lack surnames due to their solitary, non-familial lifecycle.
    *   *Examples:* Bogo, Damu, Gar, Gula, Kin, Quon, Snout, Tuog.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Polynesian, Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander mariner cultures.
*   **Culture Summary:** Reptilian humanoids who treat life as a grand, wandering journey. Born alone on remote beaches, they spend their adult lives walking the world to collect skills and sights, returning to their birthplace only at the end of their lives to lay eggs, pass down travel diaries, and pass away.

## 102. Triton
*   **Naming Conventions:** Aristocratic, long, and flowing, utilizing a strict three-syllable cadence full of liquid consonants ($M, N, L$). Male names end in vocal consonants or hard vowels; female names end in *-is* or *-ia*. Surnames are ancient noble house lineages.
    *   *Male:* Krayem, Molos, Nalos.
    *   *Female:* Aryn, Belthiss, Otavia.
    *   *House Names:* Deepwarden, Oceanstrider.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Classical Atlantean mythology and high-seas medieval chivalric hierarchies.
*   **Culture Summary:** Deep-ocean humanoids from the Elemental Plane of Water. Their culture is strictly feudal, militaristic, and somewhat haughty—having spent centuries guarding deep ocean trenches from abyssal monsters, they view themselves as the unrecognized, elite protectors of the ungrateful surface world.

## 103. Umbral Human (Changeling, Gifted)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Human given names paired with dark, somber, or shadowy surnames that reflect their migration into planar darkness.
    *   *Examples:* John Shade, Mary Void.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Dark-fantasy shadow realms and human diaspora survivalism.
*   **Culture Summary:** Humans who settled in planar shadow dimensions. Changeling variants use shadow magic to alter faces; Gifted variants possess heightened psychic intuition, running dark trading ports that connect human kingdoms with shadow economies.

## 104. Vedalken
*   **Naming Conventions:** Clinically precise, cold, and mathematically structured. They avoid explosive or emotional phonetic sounds, favoring soft sibilants, neutral plosives, and academic guild titles instead of family names.
    *   *Examples:* Agnar, Baril, Firen, Noam (Male); Donna, Imre, Janal, Rina (Female).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** High-technocracy academic guilds and Vulcan logic archetypes.
*   **Culture Summary:** Tall, blue-skinned intellectuals who believe everything can be optimized. Their culture rejects emotional displays, treating life as an objective series of systems to be continuously analyzed, cataloged, and scientifically upgraded.

## 105. Verdan
*   **Naming Conventions:** Their names are completely fluid and changing. They start life with simple, childlike goblinoid names, but mutate them throughout adulthood to match their rapid physical transformations.
    *   *Examples:* Gax, Klon, Verda, Shift.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Mutagenic science-fiction entities and corporate-satire fantasy narratives (Acquisitions Incorporated).
*   **Culture Summary:** A newborn, empathetic race mutated from goblins by a chaotic cosmic entity. Their bodies physically morph as they grow (changing size, skin color, and gender). Their culture is welcoming, communicative, and completely focused on the immediate future, free from any historical baggage or ancestral traditions.

## 106. Vulpin
*   **Naming Conventions:** Elegant, sharp, and aristocratic. They favor names that sound grand and sophisticated, paired with pompous noble titles.
    *   *Examples:* Renard, Sly, Fox, Gentry.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** European Reynard the Fox fables and eighteenth-century high-society aristocracies.
*   **Culture Summary:** Fox-like humanoids who value legal manipulation, high diplomacy, and elegant fashion. They dominate urban courts, using their silver tongues and sharp wits to rewrite local trade laws to favor their own merchant families.

## 107. Warforged
*   **Naming Conventions:** Originally assigned mechanical military serial numbers. Upon gaining freedom, they adopted short descriptive **monikers given to them by battlefield comrades**, or selected abstract noun names that represent an idealized concept they wish to study.
    *   *Examples:* Bastion, Bulwark, Rusty, Shield, Slasher (Monikers); Faith, Justice, Smith (Concepts).
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Jewish Golem folklore, Industrial Revolution machinery, and military veteran rehabilitation narratives.
*   **Culture Summary:** Sentient artificial humanoids constructed from stone, wood, and metal for a global war that has since ended. They lack historical homelands, ancestors, or biological families, instead forming emergent craftsman collectives and trying to discover a personal purpose in a world at peace.

## 108. Wechselkind
*   **Naming Conventions:** Innocent, eerie, and doll-like. They use simple, nursery-rhyme nicknames or diminutive Germanic names given to them by unsuspecting human parents.
    *   *Examples:* Hans, Greta, Doll, Poppet, Toy.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Grimms' fairy tales and European clockwork-child changeling folklore.
*   **Culture Summary:** Clockwork constructs covered in synthetic skin, built by hags to replace stolen human children. Once their artificial nature is discovered, they are abandoned, leading them to form isolated secret societies of artificial children who observe human families from afar with quiet longing.

## 109. Yuan-ti (incl. Pureblood)
*   **Naming Conventions:** Heavily sibilant, drawn-out, and intentionally snake-like, emphasizing hissing consonants ($S, Z, Sh$), glottal stops, and dragging vowels. They use temple titles instead of family names.
    *   *Examples:* Sseratus, Sszass, Shass, Xanzis, Zstis.
*   **Real-World Inspirations:** Ancient Mesoamerican religious cults (the feathered serpent temples) mixed with pulp fantasy serpent-folk tropes.
*   **Culture Summary:** A dark, caste-based society of serpentine humanoids who chemically stripped away their own human emotions to achieve cold, intellectual calculation. They operate out of hidden jungle step-pyramids, running global political conspiracies to destabilize human empires and restore their sleeping snake gods.
